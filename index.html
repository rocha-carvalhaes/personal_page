evolution, possibilities and randomness

Sometimes I catch myself wondering how is our future decided. Why do we do what we do? How many possibilities are out there? And how can we actually chase our own goals?

We're taught to study hard, work tirelessly, and make good choices. Statistically, good choices put you on a more likely path to success. But there are so many variables we can't control...

In the end, how much of our future do we really shape? Some questions raised by the book "The Drunkard's Walk", from Leonard Mlodinow.
